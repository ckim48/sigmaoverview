Project 3Sigma

The Primate Portal at Carnegie Mellon University

What is Project 3Sigma?

This project aims to deliver digital solutions for this collaborative project between Carnegie Mellon University and Rochester Institute of Technology, to facilitate the research on the origins of human thought. The deliverables will be integrated at the Seneca Park Zoo for The Primate Portal's research.

The Primate Portal

The Primate Portal, located at Carnegie Mellon University, is a project aimed to study origins and nature of thought.

Seneca Park Zoo

Partnered with the Seneca Park Zoo in Rochester, they trained a troop of Olive Baboons to solve cognitive problems with technology.

Deliverables

An overview of the deliverables produced by each team as part of the 3Sigma Capstone Program.

Psychology & Data Research

The Bio/Psychology Data Research team analyzed whether there is a correlation between learned strategies and working memory using data from The Primate Portal. Previous studies have shown that rhesus monkeys and capuchin monkeys are more adaptable to changing situations than humans, and the efficiency of using learned strategies has been suggested as the cause. The BPDR team analyzed the data received from the laboratory to verify this hypothesis, and concluded that the hypothesis is only partially valid.

UI/UX & 3D Animation

The Design & Animation team created content and artwork for The Primate Portal website. The current laboratory website provides basic functions and information, but it is not yet well organized. To provide a more elegant design and marketing effect, we created logos, 3D graphics, etc. that can be applied to websites, apps, and promotional materials. In particular, it will be used for promotional purposes for students who can utilize the laboratory's data effectively.

App Development

The Full-Stack App Dev. team developed a game app to compare cognitive abilities between human children aged 5-8 and apes. The data collection of The Primate Portal is currently limited to apes. By developing an app that can be used not only in zoos but also in public schools and homes in the United States, we can more easily record various behaviors of human users, and by providing three games, we can collect a variety of data.

Game Design

The Game Design & Dev. team developed games to be used in the laboratory's app. To collect information from various angles, a variety of types of games are needed. In addition to the three games currently used in The Primate Portal, three additional games were developed to diversify the data that can be collected.

Machine Learning

The Machine Learning & AI team developed a module for face recognition of capuchin monkeys using computers in zoos. In The Primate Portal, computer usage activities are recorded by researchers after recording the behavior of each monkey, and the data is recorded by manually identifying the individual. Rather than manually recording the results, the computer recognizes the individual monkey through the face in real time, increasing the convenience and efficiency of the study.

Robotics

The Robotics team developed a food vending machine for rewarding capuchin monkeys in zoos. The current mechanical devices used in zoos have limitations in the size of the food that can be dispensed and weak durability. To improve these shortcomings, a new vending machine design was designed using 3D printers and Arduino.
